5 Black True Crime Podcasts You Should Know

The rise in popularity for true crime is a phenomenon that few understand. Seemingly overnight, many of us, especially women, grew obsessed with true crime. There's something about diving into the twisted and baffling stories that often seem stranger than fiction. And stranger still, according to Ozy, nearly 75% of true crime podcast listeners are women. But one group is missing from the scene: Black people. As a culture, Black people have a unique way of telling stories, turning the simplest stories and weaving them into enthralling tales. But rest assured, there are a few podcasts out there!

That's why for this Black History Month we wanted to highlight five true crime podcasts by Black content creators that you should give a listen!


Black True Crime Podcast

This aptly named podcast by sisters Kayla and Kristin takes a different approach on the traditional true crime podcast format. They do it by discussing and shedding light on the lives and crimes committed by Black offenders and their victims.

To learn more about the show visit their website.


Sistas Who Kill

What's more rare than a true crime podcast with Black hosts? Try one where Black women are the ones committing the crimes. That's exactly what you'll find on this podcast by bffs MaRah and Taz. Follow them as they discuss the crimes and outcomes of Black women, including how they are treated differently in the justice system.

Check them out on Spotify.


Affirmative Murder

Amateur true crime lovers Alvin and Fran noticed that there weren't many podcasts that featured the perspective of Black 20-something millennial men, and decided to do something about it. Not only does their podcast cover minority victims and their perps, but it also does so while diving deep into the Black experience and blending gut-aching comedy.

Learn more about them on their website.


Bruh Issa Murder

The murder of his cousin is what sparked Andre's interest in true crime, but it was the lack of diversity of voices like him in the true crime community that inspired Bruh Issa Murder. Now, together with his friends Battle, Kelly, and Robert, they cover true crime cases and serial killers of color while highlighting music from lesser-known artists.

Check them out on Bruh Issa Murder.


Black Girl Gone

Tens of thousands of girls and women of color go missing every year, many of whose stories don't get love from mainstream media. Through her podcast Black Girl Gone, host Amara brings awareness to their stories by focusing on those who are missing and or murdered.

Explore the victims, their stories, and more on the Black Girl Gone website.
